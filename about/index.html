Preforms

Nepalgunj-based Bheri Hospital successfully carried out surgery of gastric cancer for the first time. Chief of the hospital Dr Prakash Thapa said a team led by gastro surgeon Dr Dhruba Sha had carried out the successful surgery of gastric cancer. The team operated on Rudra Bahadur Pun, 58, of Bheriganga Municipality. Pun was diagnosed with gastric cancer through endoscopy five months ago. He was referred to Chitwan-based Cancer Hospital. But he was again admitted to Bheri Hospital after excessive stomach pain a month ago. Anaesthesiologists Dr Paras Pandey and Tara Acharya were also in the team. The surgery was performed on November 28. Pun was discharged after 11 days. Bheri Hospital lab lacks VTM for COVID-19

NEPALGUNJ: The coronavirus test lab established at the Bheri Zonal Hospital, Nepalgunj has faced a shortage of Viral Transport Medium (VTM). VTM contains antifungal and antibiotic supplements, used in the viral test. The VTM is required for testing swab of suspected patients but the lab is facing a shortage of it, said the officiating chief of the hospital Dr. Prakash Thapa. Thapa added that they were continuing testing via PCR only for extremely needed cases due to a lack of VTM to test from other methods of the suspect ones. Thapa further shared that request has been made for additional VTM from State and central level but it is yet to be received.
